SDG 15: Life on Land

Protect, restore, and promote the sustainable use of terrestrial and forest ecosystems.

Why is Life on Land important?

Terrestrial and forest ecosystems provide essential services, including air and water purification, soil formation, and climate regulation. They are also home to a vast array of biodiversity, with many species found nowhere else on Earth.

Terrestrial ecosystems, such as forests, grasslands, and deserts, support a wide range of plant and animal life. They play a critical role in regulating the climate, producing oxygen, and providing habitat for countless species.

Wetland Ecosystems

Wetlands, including swamps, marshes, and bogs, are unique ecosystems that provide habitat for a diverse range of plants and animals. They help to filter water, prevent flooding, and support fisheries.

Wetlands are crucial for maintaining water quality, preventing soil erosion, and supporting biodiversity. They also provide important ecosystem services, such as carbon sequestration and climate regulation.

Forest Ecosystems

Forests are vital for life on Earth. They absorb carbon dioxide, regulate the climate, provide habitat for countless species, and supply timber, fuel, and other resources.

Forests play a crucial role in maintaining biodiversity, protecting watersheds, and mitigating climate change. They also provide numerous benefits to human societies, including food security, medicine, and recreation.

Threats to Life on Land

Deforestation, land degradation, and habitat destruction are major threats to terrestrial and forest ecosystems. Human activities such as agriculture, urbanization, and infrastructure development have led to widespread habitat loss and fragmentation.

Take Action

Learn more about conservation efforts and take action to protect our planet's biodiversity. Support organizations working to protect and restore ecosystems, and make sustainable choices in your daily life.
